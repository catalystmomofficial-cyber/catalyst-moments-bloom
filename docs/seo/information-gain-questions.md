# Information Gain — Questions Only YOU Can Answer

Generated with the `information-gain-prompt` skill. The blog drafts now carry
strong *process detail*, *contrarian takes*, and *specific numbers* — but the
highest form of information gain is first-hand experience, and that can only
come from you. I will never invent stories or fake data on your behalf.

Answer any of these in a voice note or a few sentences, and I'll weave them
into the matching post. Even ONE real story per post beats a thousand words
of polish.

## Birth ball posts
- What did YOU experience using a birth ball in your own pregnancy? Which
  movement actually helped, and which did nothing?
- What's the most common question moms in your community ask about birth
  balls — and what do you tell them?

## Diastasis recti / core posts
- What was your own gap measurement postpartum, and how did it change over
  which weeks? (A real "week 0 → week 8" number is gold.)
- What's one exercise you personally had to shelve and re-test back in later?
- From Core Restore Foundations participants: what's the most common mistake
  you see in week one?

## C-section post
- If you or close community members had a cesarean: what surprised you most
  in recovery that no one warned you about?

## Nutrition posts
- What's YOUR actual 3am snack? What worked when you were nursing?
- A food "supply tip" you tried that did nothing — name it.

## Self-care post
- Your real morning: what does five minutes of self-care actually look like
  in your house? Specifics beat ideals.
- What did burnout actually feel like for you, and what was the first thing
  that helped?

## TTC posts
- What tracking method did you (or your community members) use, and what
  mistake did you make in the first months?
- What do moms in the Catalyst community say surprised them most about TTC?

## How to use this file
Reply with answers in any form. Each answer becomes an "In my experience" /
"What moms in our community tell us" block in the matching post — the kind of
paragraph no AI-written competitor can copy.
